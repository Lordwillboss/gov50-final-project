Introduction

In which battleground state was general election and primary election polling the most accurate? Which state was the most inaccurate? My project uses data to explore President Bidens actual election results vs.average polling results going into the 2020 Presidential Primaries and the 2020 General Election. My hypothesis is that the polling error was largest in Georgia, as many polls in the lead up to the General election did not have Biden competing with Trump, and, anecdotally, the state hasnt voted for a Democratic presidential Candidate since Jimmy Carter in 1976.

In order to more closely analyze the error in a manner that provides meaningful results on how close/far the polls were, Im choosing to focus on 6 different states commonly agreed on as so-called swing states: North Carolina, Georgia, Arizona, Pennsylvania, Michigan, and Wisconsin. The reason for this is that if polling was off by 10 points in a state like Wyoming, it wouldnt have any substantial impact on the national election results; on the other hand, many states in both the 2020 general election and 2020 presidential primaries were decided by less than 0.1 points.

The explanatory variables Im studying are both the average polling result, and the actual election outcome. My outcome variable is polling error, or the difference between the actual election result and the aggregate/average of pre-election polls. An observed pattern that would support my hypothesis would be an average mean in polling error for GA being higher than the other battleground states, however, any mean that is higher in the other states would disprove my hypothesis.

For my data set, I will be using the 538 election analysis data for polling information for the 2020 election, and 538 data for the results of the 2020 election. The 538 data set for polling contains roughly 29,000 polls from all 50 states, while the 538 data set for results contains 7,400 official results by state for various different elections.

The independent variable of actual results is measured after each state holds its respective election, and tabulates the official vote counts for the different candidates. The dependent variable, on the other hand, is the calculated percent error, which is found by taking the aggregate of each swing states polls for their respective elections, and subtracting that from the actual election results; the dependent variable, polls, is calculated using a survey method, and my research design utilizes a cross-sectional research design method, as it is comparing the percent errors across states.

Conclusion

With regards to this research studys hypothesis, I originally hypothesized that Georgia was going to have the Largest Polling error, on the basis that a Democrat hadnt carried, or won the state in decades at the presidential level, and polling in the lead up to the election did not make it appear like President Biden was going to win the state. While my hypothesis was proven correct in that Georgia had the largest polling error, my reasoning was incorrect. As the last graph above shows, Georgia polling was incredibly inaccurate for the Primary election, but was not the most inaccurate for the general election polling. Some limitations of this analysis include the fact that the 2020 Democratic presidential primary race changes pretty dramatically in the span of 7 days at the end of February and into Early March, long after some of the primary polls had already been conducted, which are included in this dataset. As a result of the 7 day span, President Biden rapidly consolidated support in a manner that public polling wasnt able to accurately reflect for some time, and even when it did, the old polls averages were still included on the data conclusions reached in this study. Finally, another potential limitation could be the removal of missing values at various stages of this research study, which can lead to polling errors including under/over representation and reduced precision. If I had more resources, I wouldve expanded the data to include an analysis of all 50 states, not just the 6 battleground states.
